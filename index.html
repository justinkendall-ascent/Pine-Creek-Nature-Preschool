Our Story

Where the Creek Meets the Classroom

Pine Creek Nature Preschool was founded by Emily Denee, a Baker City educator and mother who believes the most profound learning happens outside — in the mud, beside the creek, and beneath the wide Oregon sky.

Emily spent years teaching in traditional classrooms before watching her own children come alive in unstructured outdoor play. That experience planted a seed. She set out to create a small, intentional program where children could spend their early years the way nature intended: exploring, building, getting dirty, and learning to move through the world with curiosity and confidence.

Today, Pine Creek is a licensed child care program welcoming preschool-age children to our outdoor classroom on Pocahontas Road. We follow the seasons, tend our worm boxes, build gnome and fairy houses in the trees, and play — rain or shine — every single day.

How We Learn

Our Philosophy

We believe children are born naturalists — endlessly curious, endlessly capable. Our job is to get out of the way and let the forest do its work.

Outdoor Immersion

The natural world is our classroom, every day and in every season. Dirt, sticks, rocks, water, and living things are our materials. We don't wait for perfect weather — we dress for it.

Child-Led Discovery

We follow children's curiosity rather than a scripted curriculum. When a child stops to watch an ant carry a crumb, that is science. When they build a fairy house from bark and moss, that is engineering and art.

Whole-Child Growth

Social, emotional, physical, and cognitive development happen together, not in isolation. Building resilience, learning to read risk, making friends, and caring for living things — these are the lessons that last a lifetime.

Environmental Stewardship

We tend worm boxes, explore seasonal cycles, and talk about why the creek matters. We want children to grow up knowing the land as a neighbor worth caring for — not just a backdrop.

What We Offer

Programs

Our small, age-grouped programs allow each child to move at their own pace while building deep friendships with a consistent community of peers.

Little Roots

Ages 2 – 3

Our youngest explorers begin building a relationship with the outdoors in a gentle, nurturing setting. Small group sizes ensure every child feels safe, seen, and celebrated.

  • Schedule: Tuesday & Thursday mornings
  • Hours: 8:30 – 11:30 am
  • Class size: Up to 6 children
  • Focus: Sensory play, nature walks, language & song

Sprouts

Ages 3 – 4

Sprouts are ready to roam further, ask bigger questions, and build longer-lasting friendships. This program leans into imaginative play, creek exploration, and collaborative projects like our famous gnome and fairy village.

  • Schedule: Monday – Thursday
  • Hours: 8:30 am – 12:00 pm
  • Class size: Up to 8 children
  • Focus: Imaginative play, loose parts, seasonal study

Explorers

Ages 4 – 5

Our Explorers are preparing for the wider world — and they're doing it through the forest. Five mornings a week they deepen their nature knowledge, practice early literacy and numeracy in context, and develop the confidence to take on challenges.

  • Schedule: Monday – Friday
  • Hours: 8:30 am – 12:00 pm
  • Optional: Lunch Bunch until 1:30 pm
  • Focus: Kindergarten readiness, leadership, stewardship

All programs are held outdoors, rain or shine. Please dress your child in weather-appropriate layers and sturdy footwear every day.
